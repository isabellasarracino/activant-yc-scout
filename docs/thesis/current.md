## Core thesis

Activant invests in commerce infrastructure — technology platforms that
help companies make, move, and sell more efficiently. The firm looks for
data-heavy, defensible business models: embedded/platform dynamics, network
effects, and marketplace mechanics, rather than point-solution tools with
weak retention.

## Priority sectors

- Fintech and embedded payments
- E-commerce and retail infrastructure
- Supply chain and logistics technology
- Insurance technology
- Healthcare infrastructure and vertical health software
- Vertical SaaS more broadly, where a workflow becomes the system of record
- AI/ML and automation applied to the operational core of a business (not
  just a thin AI wrapper on an existing workflow)

## What "escape velocity" means here

Activant's own framing of an investable company is two-sided: (1) the
company shows potential to reach escape velocity and transform its
industry, and (2) the founders can credibly lead the company through
hyper-growth, with Activant's operational support able to meaningfully
improve the odds. Rubric 2's "category-defining potential" dimension is
built around the first half of that; rubric 1 (team & general interest) is
built around the second.

## How to read this against a YC-stage company

Activant is a growth-stage investor (typically Series B-E) — nothing in a
YC batch is investable today by that standard. Scoring a YC company against
this thesis is a top-of-funnel exercise: flagging founders and companies
worth tracking now, for a growth-stage conversation years out, not a
live investment screen. Score generously on sector/business-model fit even
for a two-person pre-seed team; the team-readiness-for-growth-capital
question is out of scope at this stage by definition.
